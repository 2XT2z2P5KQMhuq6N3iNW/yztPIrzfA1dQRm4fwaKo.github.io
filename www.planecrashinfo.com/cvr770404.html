April 4, 1977
New Hope, Georgia
Southern Airways, Flight 242
McDonnell Douglas DC-9-31
N1335U

The aircraft sustained a broken windshield and loss of power to both engines after
penetrating a thunderstorm and encountering hail. The plane crash landed on a highway
and exploded in flames. Total and unique loss of thrust from both engines while the
aircraft was penetrating an area of severe thunderstorms with heavy precipitation and hail.
Conntributing factor was the company's dispatcher to provide the flight crew with up-to-
date severe weather information. Sixty-two out of 85 aboard were killed.
